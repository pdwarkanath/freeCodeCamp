Mahatma Gandhi

The man behind India's freedom struggle

Mahatma Gandhi believed in simple living. He wore clothes made of Khadi (handspun Indian textile).

  • 1969 - Mohandas Gandhi was born in Porbandar to Putlibai and Karamchand.
  • 1893 - Reached Durban, Natal. Refused to remove his Paghadi; left the court. Thrown off the first class carriage of the train at Pietermartizburg Station.
  • 1906 - Advent of Satyagraha at Empire Theatre, Johannesburg, where people took a pledge with God as their witness, to oppose the Black Act.
  • 1908 - Awarded two months' rigourous imprisonment for entry into the Transvaal without permit.
  • 1917 - Sabarmati Ashram established.
  • 1922 - Non-co-operation movement launched. Arrested for sedition.
  • 1930 - Dandi March. Broke the Salt Law. Arrested and sent to Yeravada prison.
  • 1932 - Congress Working Committee adopted the resolution of Civil Disobedience.
  • 1933 - Commneced publication of 'Harijan'. 'Harijan Sevak' (Hindi), commenced publication on 23rd February and 'Harijanbandhu' (Gujarati) commenced publication on 12th March.
  • 1942 - 'Quit India' resolution adopted; gave the call 'Do or Die'. Gandhiji and Congress Working Committee members arrested; Gandhiji imprisoned at the Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1947 - India wins independence.
  • 1948 - Assassinated by Nathuram Godse
